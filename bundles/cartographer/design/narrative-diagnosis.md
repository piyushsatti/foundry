# cartographer — narrative diagnosis (post-round-4)

*Why the round-4 render of Mock D fails Pi's glance test, and what the map's DATA must additionally carry. Hypothesis confirmed: the failure is primarily a data problem. The schema records what happened (moves, decisions, provenance, topology) but never why anything happened (intent) or what it yielded (artifacts) — topology without motive or payoff cannot be rendered into a story by any amount of ink.*

Iteration-4's own acceptance bar — "Line M alone tells the story" — is met only in the weak sense that M's *titles* occur in story order; story = goal + acts + payoffs, and all three are absent from the data.

## Part 3 — The F3 continuity failure

Why drilling reads as a context switch, per iteration-4's own contract: SECTOR **replaces** the map (a "place" in L-A/L-B at ~680px measure); the persistent landmark is the rail; the breadcrumb grows a line label ("▸ ① Mainline — review orchestration, plan, execution"). So on entering F3 the viewer loses the topology (forks, merges, the other four lines, position-in-whole) and keeps only the rail — which encodes *transcript time*, the one ordering the map deliberately does not use. Three coordinate systems with no shared frame: map = spine/topology space, sector = reading-column space, rail = chronology space. Pi's "it just switches me from the timeline to something else" is literally accurate: the drill swaps coordinate systems instead of magnifying one. And the data gap compounds it — even a perfectly continuous zoom would land on n07@v2's card unable to say *why n07 matters to the session*, because intent, beat, and artifact context don't exist to display.

**Continuity mechanisms:**

1. **Zoom-into-place (focus+context).** SECTOR stops being a place: the clicked line's column expands to reading measure *in situ* while the other lines compress to slim live strokes at the margins, fork/merge runs still attached. You zoom INTO the map; the rest compresses, never vanishes. *Cost: the hardest layout of the three (animated column expansion, reflow) and it amends the "layers are an IA, not a zoom dial" ruling.*
2. **Persistent story spine.** A slim strip under the masthead, identical in every layer: intent sentence (#4) · beat bands (#5) · frontier caret (#2) · current node/line highlighted. The narrative frame travels into the sector even though the geometry doesn't. *Cost: cheap to render, but it is exactly Part 2's data made visible — without additions 2/4/5 it has nothing to say.*
3. **Panel-not-place, extended.** Kill sector-as-place: SECTOR is the node panel maximized — a wide overlay above the dimmed, unmoved OUTLINE hub (L-C's existing answer), with the focused line's fork/merge stubs drawn in the overlay gutter so topology follows you in. *Cost: cheapest — L-C is already spec'd; known trade-off is long lines scrolling inside a lightbox.*

**Pick: mechanism 2**, with 3 as its structural complement (i.e., L-C plus the story spine). Mechanism 2 works under whichever of L-A/L-B/L-C wins, and it is the render-side twin of this file's diagnosis: continuity fails because the story frame isn't data — carry the data, and the frame carries itself.

---

**Bottom line:** the map faithfully draws a session whose goal, acts, payoffs, and settlements were never captured — fix the reducer's data contract (artifacts, frontier, line-because, intent, beats) before spending another rendering round, because no view of mock-d.json as it stands can answer "what was this session for and what did it produce."
