## Constraints discovered (research-backed, feed Phase 2/3 — not yet Pi-ratified decisions)
- **Outdate on semantic change only, not any update.** GitHub's outdated mechanic transplants, but its worst-reviewed behavior is outdating on *any* push. Consequence: the schema needs element-level stable IDs (intra-node claims/assumptions), not just node@version. (ideation-patterns, Phase 2/3)
- **History retention is load-bearing, not housekeeping.** Cartographer owns its node store (unlike Hypothesis) — immutable, never-GC'd node versions make annotation orphaning nearly impossible *by construction*. (Phase 3 schema)
- **Gerrit ported-comments beats outdated-alone:** unresolved annotations follow the node forward; resolved ones stay behind at the version they addressed. Belongs in the Phase 2 prototype. (Phase 2)
- **Honest orphan > confident wrong reattachment** (Brush et al.) — if an annotation can't be confidently re-anchored, show it orphaned with its original context, never guess. (Phase 2)

- Compaction-seam questions from mock-c (feed into Phase 1 designs + Phase 3 schema; detail in `mocks/mock-c.md`):
  - The seam is chronological but the spine isn't — seam may need to be a per-version property of nodes, not a position in the spine.
  - Provenance likely needs a `kind` discriminator (raw-span vs compaction-summary); raw JSONL still exists on disk even when the reducer can't see it.
  - The MAGE-style validate pass breaks at the seam unless it may re-open raw transcript from disk.
  - Phase 2's outdated-annotation diffs can cross the seam (n01@2→@3), so the mechanic can't assume uniform provenance.

- Merge/diverge findings from mock-d (feed Phase 3 schema + Phase 2; detail in `mocks/mock-d.md`):
  - Merges make the map a **DAG**; the parent-tree schema can't express it (n07: one parent, four incoming lines, one returning). Fork/merge edges: on lines, on nodes, or first-class edge objects?
  - Merge edges may need to target **node@version** — MERGE-2 lands as a version bump on its own fork point; merge-as-update vs merge-as-new-node is currently indistinguishable in JSON.
  - Fork *cause* is prose, not structure (FORK-2 exists because of a finding inside n07's summary) — connects to Phase 2's selectable-assumption mechanic.
  - Single `forks_from`/`merges_into` fields can't express merge-and-re-emerge lines → per-line edge lists.
  - **The verbatim-quote rule needs a fallback for gate-approved decisions** — plan-mode UI approvals leave no user prose to quote (n15).
  - **Provenance reveals what summaries scrub**: sanitized hostnames live at the very lines provenance points to; drill-through would expose them. Real privacy design question for Phase 3.
  - Line budget: the real session had a sixth line-pair, folded to stay at 5 — busy sessions may routinely exceed any fixed line budget; folding policy needed.
- Schema-v2 friction from mock-d2 (Phase 3; detail in `mocks/mock-d2.md`): line succession ≠ fork/merge (lines have lifecycles); line-`because` needs a verbatim fallback (same class as UI-gate decisions); demoted structure re-emerges as segment topology; `produced_by` can't record later artifact updates (needed `updated_by`); artifact status needed `in-progress` for mid-write capture; beats ≅ lines in act-sequential sessions (near-redundant here); intent-shift anchoring needs an explicit reducer rule (anchor to decisions, not findings); moment classification fits container nodes poorly; conditional decisions could auto-expire (n02 contained its own expiry condition).
