## Implications for manifold

**Lean into ÔÇťintent drift,ÔÇŁ but define it more concretely than the market does.** The term is now legible across PM/spec/code tools, enterprise testing, and agent-security vendors, but the meanings are drifting. Manifold should explicitly anchor it to a specific failure mode: *code that is valid, test-green, and even review-approved, yet no longer matches the preserved why*. That is crisp, memorable, and already validated by Stonewall and Tricentis. ţłÇciteţłéturn29view0ţłéturn12search0ţłéturn30search24ţłü

**Avoid positioning as another PRD generator, requirements manager, or ÔÇťAI PM copilot.ÔÇŁ** Community evidence shows people are tired of static documents, fragmented tools, and PRDs that become theater. Stonewall, Omniflow, and PM community threads all point to the same market skepticism: drafting is easy, keeping intent alive is hard. Manifold should sound like a **durable intent substrate** or **project compass**, not a faster document writer. ţłÇciteţłéturn18search14ţłéturn18search0ţłéturn18search3ţłéturn3search0ţłéturn28view0ţłü

**Differentiate from validation tools by owning rationale, not just alignment.** RealityCheck, drift-detect, Roady, Qase, and ReqToCode all validate some slice of spec/plan/test/code fit. What remains under-served is the preserved *reason* for work across months: customer signal, decision lineage, rejected alternatives, and change reasons.
